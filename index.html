Frequently asked

How is this different from SEO?

SEO optimizes for rankings on the open internet — keywords, backlinks, content volume. It works because search engines crawl in real time and rank what they find. AI models don't work that way. They don't search. They recall. When ChatGPT or Perplexity answers a question about your business, it's drawing on a knowledge graph built from structured signals across the web — not running a live search. We improve the odds of that recall by engineering the signals that shape how AI constructs and classifies your entity. That's a different problem, at a different layer.

My marketing agency already handles our online presence. Why do I need this?

Keep the relationship — if they understand the problem, by all means work with them. The issue isn't willingness. Any agency can package this and charge for it. The issue is capability. Their entire methodology is built for a different retrieval paradigm — content, backlinks, social signals. That's the muscle they've developed, and they're good at it. Entity coherence at the structured signal layer requires a different toolkit and a different mental model. If they take it on, they're learning on your dime while continuing to sell you what they already know how to do.

What exactly is entity coherence, and why does it matter for AI retrieval?

When an AI model answers a question about your business confidently, it isn't just reading your website. It borrows and corroborates trust from across the open internet — forming a knowledge graph. Your website says you're the best. But what does the rest of the internet say? What proof exists across directories, registries, citations, and structured data that corroborates that claim? The degree to which all those signals consistently answer the same questions — what is this business, who runs it, where is it, is it credentialed — is entity coherence. Low coherence means the model hedges or skips you. High coherence means it retrieves you with confidence.
